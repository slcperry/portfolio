Chicago Reader - Audience Research

The Chicago Reader is the city's oldest alternative magazine. The paper teamed up with Medill's Local News Accelerator to research their readership and expand print distribution. As a fellow, I designed and implemented our qualitative reserach methods, street interviews in various neighborhoods and a digital Qualtrics survey. We cross-referenced our findings with a quantitative analysis of expenditure spending by geography and Nielsen-Scarborough consumer reports to draft nuanced audience personas. Our research revealed key insights about reader preferences and informed distribution strategy.

Chicago Metropolitan Sports Association (IMC 307) - Brand Positioning

Chicago Metropolitan Sports Assocation is the largest non-profit LGBTQ+ sports organization in the Midwest. As part of an advanced marketing course, I collaborated with a team of students to design a comprehensive digital communications strategy for CMSA. We leveraged our industry and competitor analysis with organizational data to define a target market and create audience personas. These insights informed our brand positioning recommendations, and digital communication strategies. Referencing Google, Youtube, and Momentum Marketing tactics, we developed a 52-week content calendar, hub content pillars, help content topics, hero promotional ideas, and budgeting plans. Ultimately our recommendations will help CMSA becoming a reference point for Chicagoans seeking an inclusive community. In addition, our search engine optimization reccomendations will transform their digital platforms into a welcoming interactive and informative community.

Strategic Communications (IMC 306) - Crisis Management

Crises are unpredictable—although preventable! — events that threaten a brand’s reputation. It is important that brands swiftly address these situations with genuine concern to rebuild trust with their audience. Monitoring and controlling the narrative is imperative, after pausing any regularly scheduled communication.

SwiftEats is a fictional food delivery undergoing an employee abuse scandal. This crisis management plan allows SwiftEats to authentically rebuild trust while staying true to the vision, mission, and values of the brand.

This assignment demonstrates my familiarity with crisis management and my understanding of an appropriate response.
